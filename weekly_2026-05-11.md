## LANCET

### L1. Incident Diabetes After Switching to INSTIs in People with HIV
- **Citation:** Hwang YJ, Lesko CR, Brown TT, Alexander GC, et al. *Lancet HIV.* 2026;13(5):e297–e305.
- **Design:** Target trial emulation; sequential; IPTW with time-varying weights
- **Data:** 27 pooled longitudinal HIV cohorts, USA + Canada; 13,071 participants; 2016–2022
- **Key findings:** Switching from PI → INSTI: HR 1.38 (1.06–1.80) for incident diabetes; risk concentrated in first 2 years (HR 1.67); not explained by weight gain. Switching from NNRTI → INSTI: HR 1.10 (NS).
- **Relevance:** Largest multi-cohort TTE in HIV pharmacoepidemiology; disentangles weight-mediated vs weight-independent metabolic risk of INSTIs — directly relevant to prescribing decisions for >38 million people globally on ART.
- **File:** `papers/LANCET/LANCET_01_INSTI_diabetes_HIV_2026.md`

### L2. Discontinuing Anticholinesterase Treatment and Cognitive Decline in Alzheimer's Disease
- **Citation:** Lecerf S, Guinebretiere O, Bentegeac R, et al. *Lancet Regional Health – Europe.* 2026;62:101607.
- **Design:** Quasi-experiment + TTE (sequential, 12 monthly trials); IPTW; ITT analog; open access
- **Data:** French BNA database (n=7,319) for cognition; Meotis database (n=829) for mortality; 2017–2024
- **Key findings:** ChEI discontinuation → MMSE worse by 0.97 points at 1 year (p<0.001) and 1.81 points at 4 years (p<0.001). No mortality difference (RR 1.10, NS). Effect equivalent to 6–12 month delay in cognitive decline.
- **Relevance:** Exploits France's 2018 national ChEI delisting as a natural experiment — rare real-world stop-trial emulation; argues strongly for policy reversal. Exemplar of quasi-experimental TTE leveraging natural policy variation.
- **File:** `papers/LANCET/LANCET_02_ChEI_Alzheimer_France_2026.md`

## BMJ

### B1. GLP-1 RA Discontinuation and Risk of MACE — Duration-Response Analysis
- **Citation:** Xie Y, Choi T, Al-Aly Z. *BMJ Medicine.* 2026;5(1):e002150. PMC13007077.
- **Design:** TTE with 16 pre-specified treatment strategies; marginal structural model (MSM); time-varying IPTW; open access
- **Data:** US Veterans Affairs national EHR; GLP-1RA initiators n=132,551; sulfonylurea n=201,136; 2017–2023
- **Key findings:** Continuous 3-year GLP-1RA use: IRR 0.82 (0.78–0.85) vs sulfonylurea. Even 0.5-year discontinuation increased MACE risk by 4% (IRR 1.04) vs continued use; 2-year gap: IRR 1.22. Minimum ~2 years required for significant MACE reduction.
- **Relevance:** First study to quantify the dose-time relationship between GLP-1RA continuity and CV protection; critical for managing the ~50–80% real-world discontinuation rate. MSM with 16 strategies is a methodological template for treatment persistence research.
- **File:** `papers/BMJ/BMJ_01_GLP1_discontinuation_MACE_2026.md`

### B2. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists and Cause-Specific Acute Pancreatitis — TTE
- **Citation:** Xie Y, Choi T, Al-Aly Z. *BMJ Medicine.* 2026;5(1):e002183. PMC13110570.
- **Design:** TTE; ITT + per-protocol; IPW; discrete-time survival models; cause-specific outcome classification; open access
- **Data:** Same VA cohort as B1 (n=333,687); 12-month follow-up; 2017–2023
- **Key findings:** All-cause pancreatitis: null (RD −3.64 per 100,000; NS). Opposing effects: drug-induced pancreatitis ↑ (+23.45), hypertriglyceridaemia-associated ↓ (−16.96), alcohol-induced ↓ (−10.32). Drug-induced events cluster early (42% in first 3 months); protective effects accrue months 4–12.
- **Relevance:** Resolves the longstanding GLP-1 RA pancreatitis controversy by showing cause-specific opposing effects. Addresses UK MHRA safety investigation (June 2025). Methodologically notable: cause-specific pancreatitis subtyping via NLP + lab data is a model for future drug safety studies.
- **File:** `papers/BMJ/BMJ_02_GLP1_pancreatitis_2026.md`

### B3. High-Dose Folic Acid Timing and Congenital Anomalies in Women on Antiseizure Medications
- **Citation:** Sun Y, Alvestad S, Cohen JM, et al. *JNNP (BMJ).* 2026. doi:10.1136/jnnp-2025-337395.
- **Design:** Sequential TTE (weekly nested trials); IPTW; Nordic nationwide registers; open access (CC BY-NC 4.0)
- **Data:** Denmark, Iceland, Norway, Sweden registers; 1997–2020; 18,255 / 13,619 / 12,365 eligible pregnancies by timing window
- **Key findings:** Folic acid initiation 1–12 weeks before pregnancy: RR 0.55 (0.25–0.91), ARD −2.2 pp (−3.5 to −0.5) — 45% relative risk reduction in MCA. Initiation after pregnancy onset: no benefit (RR 1.27, NS). Very early initiation (13–52 weeks before): no benefit overall.
- **Relevance:** Most rigorous evidence to date on a widely debated clinical recommendation; shows the timing window matters critically. Pre-registered; multi-country Nordic registers = near-complete ascertainment. Sequential TTE applied to reproductive pharmacoepidemiology — a methodologically under-explored area.
- **File:** `papers/BMJ/BMJ_03_folic_acid_ASM_congenital_2026.md`

## JAMA

### J1. Discontinuation of RAS Inhibitors After Acute eGFR Decline — TTE
- **Citation:** Ku E, Tangri N, Brar R, McCulloch CE, et al. *JAMA Network Open.* 2026.
- **Design:** TTE; propensity score matching (1:2); ITT; Cox PH; Manitoba population-based EHR + linked provincial lab data
- **Data:** Manitoba Centre for Health Policy; n=4,233 (1,411 discontinuers; 2,822 continuers); 2008–2021
- **Key findings:** RASI discontinuation after ≥15% eGFR decline → HR 1.74 (1.28–2.38) for ESKD; HR 1.23 (1.07–1.41) for death. No significant difference in MACE (1.13, NS) or AKI (1.11, NS). 33% of patients discontinued — a common but harmful practice.
- **Relevance:** Directly challenges the common clinical reflex to stop RAS inhibitors when eGFR dips; provides actionable evidence that continuation is safer. TTE with provincial lab linkage enables precise eGFR-based eligibility — a model for kidney pharmacoepidemiology.
- **File:** `papers/JAMA/JAMA_01_RAS_inhibitor_eGFR_kidney_2026.md`

### J2. Ventricular Arrhythmia Risk with Antipsychotic + SSRI Co-prescription — Sequential TTE
- **Citation:** Chien HT, Lin SW, Kung TJ, Wang CC, et al. *JAMA Network Open.* 2026.
- **Design:** Sequential TTE (52 weekly trials); per-protocol + IPTW + IPCW; time-varying covariates; US + Taiwan replication
- **Data:** US insurance claims 2010–2023 (n=307,818; 4.37M person-trials); Taiwan NHIRD 2010–2021 (n=191,080; 2.15M person-trials)
- **Key findings:** SSRI initiation in antipsychotic users → HR 1.51 (1.04–2.19) in US; HR 3.32 (2.26–4.88) in Taiwan. Citalopram: HR 2.20; escitalopram: HR 2.84. Positive/negative controls validate approach.
- **Relevance:** First application of sequential TTE to a drug-drug interaction (DDI) question — a major methodological contribution. Two-country replication with positive/negative controls sets a new standard for DDI safety research. Clinically important for the millions co-prescribed these agents.
- **File:** `papers/JAMA/JAMA_02_antipsychotic_SSRI_arrhythmia_2026.md`

### J3. Low-Dose Aspirin for CVD Prevention in Giant Cell Arteritis — Clone-Censor-Weight TTE
- **Citation:** Beydon M, Hajage D, Guédon AF, Carrat F, et al. *JAMA Network Open.* 2026.
- **Design:** TTE with **clone-censor-weight (CCW)** approach; IPCW; French SNDS population-based data
- **Data:** French National Health Data System; n=14,528 incident GCA patients; 2010–2022; 36% aspirin initiators
- **Key findings:** 1-year MACE: RR 0.86 (0.75–0.96); RD −0.54%. Major hemorrhage: RR 1.29 (1.05–1.53); RD +0.51%. 3-year MACE: RD −1.08% (persistent benefit). Stronger in women (RD −0.78%) and diabetics (RD −2.23%).
- **Relevance:** One of the few applied TTE studies using the full clone-censor-weight design (not just single time-zero). Addresses a genuine evidence gap where RCTs are ethically and logistically infeasible. Provides first real-world evidence for aspirin in GCA — a population with ~3× baseline CV risk.
- **File:** `papers/JAMA/JAMA_03_aspirin_GCA_CVD_2026.md`

## Cross-Journal Trend Notes (2026-05-11)

1. **Clone-censor-weight (CCW) is moving from theory to practice.** The GCA aspirin paper (JAMA) is one of the first high-profile applied studies to use the full CCW design. This signals the method is maturing from methodological exposition into routine pharmacoepidemiology.

2. **Sequential TTE is now the default for time-varying drug initiation questions.** Three papers this week (antipsychotic+SSRI, nursing home influenza, babesiosis) all use sequential TTE — it is becoming the standard rather than the exception for drug safety questions where initiation timing varies.

3. **The Al-Aly VA group (GLP-1 RA × 2 papers) is defining the GLP-1 RA real-world evidence agenda.** Both the MACE discontinuation and cause-specific pancreatitis papers use the same 333K-person VA cohort with 16 pre-specified strategies — a template others will follow for any treatment-persistence question.

4. **Nordic register + sequential TTE is the gold standard for reproductive pharmacoepidemiology.** The folic acid/ASM paper shows how near-complete Nordic registers enable pre-registered, multi-country sequential TTE for rare, sensitive outcomes (MCAs in pregnancy) — a design almost impossible in other healthcare systems.

5. **Causal inference is moving into HIV and autoimmune disease.** Historically concentrated in cardiovascular and cancer pharmacoepidemiology, TTE is now being applied to HIV ART switching (2 Lancet HIV papers), autoimmune disease (GCA aspirin, giant cell arteritis), and Alzheimer's (French natural experiment) — broadening its clinical footprint.
