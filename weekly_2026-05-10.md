- **Citation:** Chien HT, Lin SW, Kung TJ, Wang CC, et al. *JAMA Network Open.* 2026.

### 5. Tailored Exercise Strategies and Mortality in Breast Cancer Survivors — TTE

- **Citation:** Wang C, Tang D, Peter D, Ju C, Liu L, Wang Y, et al. *The BMJ.* 2026.

- **Relevance:** Critical validation paper for the TTE field — directly informs when and how much to trust TTE estimates.

## Trend Notes (May 2026)

1. **Sequential TTE is becoming mainstream.** Three of the papers above explicitly use sequential (nested) target trial emulation — not just single-index TTE — reflecting growing awareness of immortal-time bias and its correction.

2. **HIV / antiretroviral safety is a hot application area.** Two Lancet HIV papers (INSTI diabetes, INSTI metabolic/MACE) and a metabolic editorial appeared simultaneously, suggesting a coordinated evidence push on INSTI metabolic safety using TTE.

3. **TTE validation against RCTs is intensifying.** The BMJ systematic review on TTE-RCT concordance builds on RCT-DUPLICATE and signals that the field is now mature enough to formally benchmark itself — a prerequisite for regulatory acceptance.

4. **Non-pharmacological and rare-disease TTE is expanding.** Exercise in cancer survivors (JAMA Network Open), babesiosis exchange transfusion (JAMA IM), GCA aspirin (JAMA Network Open), and nursing home influenza (JAMA IM) all apply TTE outside the traditional drug-vs-drug pharmacoepidemiology setting.

5. **AI + causal inference convergence.** The Lancet Americas review and multiple preprints signal that "causal AI" (using ML for confounding adjustment within TTE) is moving from methods papers into applied studies.
