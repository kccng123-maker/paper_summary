## BMJ (Family Journals)

### B1. Catheter Ablation and MACE Risk in Newly Diagnosed AF: A Target Trial Emulation
**Authors:** Ho CC, Lin KH, Lai CC, Tsai WW, Lu KH  
**Journal:** *Heart* (BMJ family) — published 7 May 2026  
**DOI:** 10.1136/heartjnl-2025-327113  
**File:** [BMJ_06_catheter_ablation_AF_MACE_TTE_2026.md](papers/BMJ/BMJ_06_catheter_ablation_AF_MACE_TTE_2026.md)

Using the TriNetX global federated EHR database, this TTE study identified 263,329 patients with newly diagnosed AF and matched 2,018 ablation recipients 1:1 with non-ablated controls. With a 1-year landmark design to eliminate immortal time bias, catheter ablation was associated with a 58% lower risk of MACE (HR 0.42, 95% CI 0.29–0.60) and 37% lower heart failure risk (HR 0.63, 95% CI 0.49–0.80). Results were robust in 2-year sensitivity and subgroup analyses. This is the largest TTE of catheter ablation in high-risk newly diagnosed AF to date, though the highly selected 0.8% ablation rate suggests residual confounding by indication. Full text paywalled — abstract only.

### B2. Emulated Trial of AI Use and Depressive Outcomes in US Adults
**Authors:** Perlis RH, Gunning FM, Uslu A, et al.  
**Journal:** *BMJ Mental Health* — published 13 May 2026  
**DOI:** 10.1136/bmjment-2025-302609  
**File:** [BMJ_07_AI_use_depression_TTE_2026.md](papers/BMJ/BMJ_07_AI_use_depression_TTE_2026.md)

This open-access study applied TTE to three waves of the CHIP-50 nationally representative US survey (n=19,099 at baseline; 3,109 at follow-up). High-frequency generative AI use (≥multiple times/week, 15% of sample) was compared to low-frequency use using inverse probability weighting for both treatment allocation and attrition. The primary outcome was PHQ-9 depressive symptom score change. High-frequency AI use was not associated with depressive symptoms (mean difference −0.18, 95% CI −0.94 to 0.59; p=0.65), with no subgroup heterogeneity detected by generalised causal forests (calibration p=0.81). The study directly challenges popular narratives linking AI use to depression at the population level, while noting inability to exclude harms in vulnerable subgroups not well-represented in the panel.

### B3. Morning vs Afternoon ICI Administration in Metastatic NSCLC: Target Trial Emulation
**Authors:** Gonzalez RT, Datta R, Rehmani S, Strohbehn GW, et al.  
**Journal:** *Journal for ImmunoTherapy of Cancer (JITC)* (BMJ family) — published 14 May 2026  
**DOI:** 10.1136/jitc-2025-013830  
**File:** [BMJ_08_ICI_chronotherapy_NSCLC_TTE_2026.md](papers/BMJ/BMJ_08_ICI_chronotherapy_NSCLC_TTE_2026.md)

In the largest TTE of ICI chronotherapy to date, 4,688 stage IV NSCLC patients from the national Veterans Health Administration were analysed using marginal structural models with IPCW. Patients receiving the first three ICI infusions in the AM (before noon) had median OS of 10.3 months vs 8.1 months for PM dosing (HR for PM vs AM: 1.15, 95% CI 1.04–1.26; p=0.004). A negative control chemotherapy cohort (n=7,951) showed no time-of-day effect (HR 1.05; p=0.15), supporting a specific circadian-immune mechanism rather than general confounding. The study eliminates immortal time bias and addresses longitudinal confounding — two major flaws in prior smaller studies. The finding supports an immediately actionable, zero-cost scheduling intervention pending prospective RCT confirmation.

### L1. Physical Activity and Biological Age Measured by DNA Methylation Clocks: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
**Authors:** Shan J, Tay JH, Wang W, Tan R, Joshi R, Maier AB, et al.  
**Journal:** *The Lancet Healthy Longevity* — published 29 April 2026  
**DOI:** 10.1016/S2666-7568(26)00019-X  
**File:** [LANCET_04_physical_activity_DNAmethylation_biological_age_2026.md](papers/LANCET/LANCET_04_physical_activity_DNAmethylation_biological_age_2026.md)

This PROSPERO-registered systematic review and meta-analysis (CRD42024499021) synthesised 44 studies (145,465 participants; databases searched Jan 2011–Jun 2025) on physical activity and epigenetic clock-measured biological age. Higher physical activity was associated with lower Horvath EAA (β=−0.03, 95% CI −0.05 to −0.01) and lower GrimAge EAA (β=−0.09, 95% CI −0.12 to −0.05) per 1 SD increase in MET-min/week. Hannum and PhenoAge EAA associations were non-significant. The study is methodologically notable for analysing three generations of DNAm clocks separately and prominently flagging that the predominance of cross-sectional designs limits causal inference — an honest appraisal that strengthens the call for longitudinal RCTs with objectively measured activity. This paper marks Lancet Healthy Longevity's growing agenda in geroprotection science.

## Cross-Journal Trend Notes — 2026-05-18

- **TTE applied to scheduling/timing interventions:** The ICI chronotherapy paper (BMJ_08) is the first TTE study this tracking period to use infusion timing (AM vs PM) as the treatment — expanding TTE from drug choice to delivery logistics. MSM + negative control design sets a new methodological bar.

- **AI as both study subject and methodological tool:** This week features a TTE *of* AI use (BMJ Mental Health), while prior weeks documented AI's integration as a TTE design tool (Lancet Americas narrative review). Medical journals are now using causal inference methods to rigorously evaluate AI's own health effects — a meta-methodological development.

- **Epigenetic biomarkers entering causal inference discourse:** The Lancet Healthy Longevity paper explicitly situates DNAm clock studies within a causal inference framework (distinguishing what can/cannot be inferred from cross-sectional designs), signalling that biomarker research is maturing toward causal standards.

- **Cardiac rhythm control via TTE:** Two TTE papers on cardiac arrhythmia rhythm control in recent weeks (OAC for VTE from BMJ Nov 2025; now catheter ablation for AF). TTE is displacing traditional registry analyses as the preferred design for evaluating invasive procedures where RCT evidence is sparse.

- **Non-significant TTE results publishing:** The null AI/depression TTE and the absence of NEJM TTE papers this week reflect a healthy ecosystem — TTE studies with null results are being published and valued for their methodological contribution, not just positive findings.
