## Editorial Focus & Journal Identity

JAMA is the flagship journal of the American Medical Association — focused on clinical practice guidelines, US health policy, high-impact RCTs, and evidence synthesis. In 2026, JAMA's agenda is driven by:
- **US healthcare economics and corporatisation** — management consultants in hospitals, drug pricing
- **Stroke and cardiovascular medicine** — major thrombolysis/thrombectomy trials
- **Oncology** — RAS therapeutics, cancer screening, rural/urban cancer disparities
- **Preventive medicine** — USPSTF recommendations dominating the news feed
- **GLP-1 / obesity medicine** — tirzepatide, exercise-weight management integration
- **Alzheimer's disease** — donanemab trial, drug treatment advances

## US Health Policy & Editorial Themes (2026)

### Corporatisation of Healthcare
- **Management consultants in nonprofit hospitals**: JAMA investigation — do they deliver value? Financial outcomes post-consultant engagement
- **Medicare drug pricing (Most-Favored-Nation model)**: NEJM/JAMA debate on Part B drug pricing reform
- **Medicaid pricing model**: generous MFN — promise and limitations analysis
- **Academic medicine under corporate medicine**: impacts on training (NEJM series cross-cited)

### Preventive Medicine / USPSTF
- Intimate partner violence screening
- Syphilis screening in pregnancy
- Breastfeeding support counselling
- Food insecurity screening
- Iron deficiency in pregnancy
- Falls prevention in community-dwelling older adults
- Breast cancer screening (density and masking)

### Cancer Disparities
- **Rural vs urban cancer mortality shift** — cancer deaths now more concentrated in rural US
- **Breast density masking** — precision screening need highlighted
