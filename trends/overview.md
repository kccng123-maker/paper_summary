## 2026 Cross-Journal Mega-Trends

### 1. GLP-1 / Obesity Medicine Ecosystem
All four journals are publishing extensively on GLP-1 receptor agonists:
- **Lancet**: Cardiometabolic risk in HIV on INSTI (REPRIEVE trial); GLP-1 vs SGLT2i for kidney outcomes
- **BMJ**: GLP-1 discontinuation and MACE risk (MSM TTE); GLP-1 and pancreatitis
- **JAMA**: Tirzepatide weight maintenance; "exercise conundrum in the GLP-1 era"
- **NEJM**: Tirzepatide and obesity pharmacotherapy reviews
- **Trend**: GLP-1 drugs are now so prevalent that journals are publishing on downstream consequences — what happens when you stop, what about exercise, what about organ-specific effects

### 2. Stroke / Cardiovascular Acute Care
- **JAMA**: Three concurrent thrombolysis/thrombectomy trials redefining acute ischemic stroke care (tirofiban, tenecteplase, intra-arterial alteplase)
- **NEJM**: Microaxial flow pump (Impella) in high-risk PCI; atrial fibrillation post-ablation anticoagulation
- **Lancet**: Cardiovascular outcomes in GCA; MACE endpoints dominant
- **Trend**: Major shift in acute cardiovascular care protocols happening simultaneously across multiple fronts

### 3. AI in Medicine — Growing Scepticism
- **BMJ**: "AI is making clinical reasoning optional — that should worry us"; UK Biobank AI data breach risk
- **JAMA**: "Promoting Clinical Expertise in the Age of AI"; "Elective MRI Screening — Buyer Beware" (AI screening hype)
- **NEJM**: "Teaching Anatomy — To Dissect or Not to Dissect?" (technology vs tradition)
- **Lancet**: Fabricated AI-generated citations audit across 2.5M papers
- **Trend**: Medical journals are moving from AI enthusiasm (2023–2024) to AI scepticism and harm-framing in 2026

### 4. Gene Therapy / Precision Medicine
- **NEJM**: Base editing of HBG1/HBG2 for sickle cell disease — curative approach
- **JAMA**: Daraxonrasib for RAS-mutated pancreatic cancer
- **Lancet**: Enpatoran (TLR 7/8 inhibitor) in SLE — targeted immunology
- **Trend**: Gene editing and precision-targeted biologics are entering disease areas previously considered untreatable

### 5. mRNA Vaccine Platform Expansion
- **NEJM**: mRNA seasonal influenza vaccine outperforms standard shot
- **BMJ**: mRNA flu vaccine coverage; aluminium adjuvant safety review
- **Lancet**: Malaria vaccine implementation (RTS,S — not mRNA but same vaccine-science wave)
- **Trend**: Post-COVID mRNA platform diversifying into flu, RSV, and potentially TB/malaria

### 6. Corporatisation and Politicisation of Healthcare
- **NEJM**: Commissioned series on corporate medicine in academic hospitals
- **JAMA**: Management consultants in nonprofit hospitals — outcome data
- **BMJ**: Reform UK impact on NHS; FDA political suppression of vaccine safety data
- **Trend**: Major journals increasingly covering healthcare as a political economy, not just a clinical field

### 7. Conflict and Health Systems
- **Lancet**: Lebanon, Gaza, Sudan — health system collapse
- **BMJ**: Iran/Israel war, Lebanon hidden crisis, drone strikes on hospitals
- **JAMA**: Hantavirus patient information; indirect coverage of global health insecurity
- **Trend**: Medical journals functioning as conflict health monitors — war-on-healthcare as recurring editorial theme
