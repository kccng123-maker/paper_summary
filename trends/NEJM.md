## Editorial Focus & Journal Identity

NEJM is the world's highest-impact medical journal, known for landmark RCTs, clinical reviews, and US health policy perspectives. In 2026, NEJM's agenda focuses on:
- **Cardiovascular innovation** — high-risk PCI, HFpEF, atrial fibrillation
- **Oncology** — RAS-targeted therapies in pancreatic cancer
- **Infectious disease** — mRNA flu vaccine, scabies, COVID vaccination
- **Gene therapy / base editing** — sickle cell disease
- **US healthcare economics** — corporatisation of academic medicine, drug pricing (MFN)
- **Neurology** — cerebral amyloid angiopathy, PCI-related brain events

## Note on TTE/Causal Inference in NEJM

NEJM rarely publishes TTE or applied causal inference methodology papers. Its content is dominated by RCTs and clinical reviews. For TTE content, check:
- **NEJM Evidence** — more likely to include observational/real-world evidence studies
- **NEJM Catalyst** — health delivery innovation, some causal frameworks
- NEJM proper: monitor for landmark observational studies on major policy questions (Medicaid expansion, pandemic interventions) that may use causal inference methods without explicit TTE labelling
