## LANCET

### L1. When evidence meets artificial intelligence — *The Lancet Regional Health – Americas* (2026;59:101472)
A state-of-the-art methodological Review (Cruz-Suarez et al.) examining how AI is reshaping evidence-based medicine through multimodal data integration, **target trial emulation**, and simulation-based modelling. Its central argument for this archive: AI's predictive performance does **not** guarantee causal validity, and the target trial framework is the bridge that converts large-scale observational/AI pipelines into well-defined causal questions (explicit eligibility, time zero, treatment strategies, causal contrast). It also flags representativeness/equity risks in fragmented health systems (the Americas) and calls for governance reform and transparent validation. Useful as a conceptual companion to applied TTE work and to the recent BMJ TTE-vs-RCT concordance papers.
